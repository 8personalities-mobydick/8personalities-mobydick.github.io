Personalities The Test About

Your personality type is:

Ishmael

Ishmaels are wind-blown adventurers, ready to conquer anything with patience and a level head.

“As for me, I am tormented with an everlasting itch for things remote. I love to sail forbidden seas, and land on barbarous coasts.”

Introduction

Ishmaels are driven by a desire to explore and understand the unknown; they don’t want to look at the sea from the dock, but rather sail out into the sea to experience the watery world for themselves. Rather than taking agency and deciding their life for themselves, however, they believe that it’s fate that brings them these opportunities and experiences.

They view life through a philosophical, analytical lens; for Ishmaels, the hunt for a whale is about the obsessive tendencies in human nature rather than simply a career, and the monkey-rope upon a ship is more representative of intertwined human relationships than a practical tool. Such reflection brings new colors and depths to Ishmaels’ lives and offers them unique perspectives.

While they prefer to take a less assertive role on a ship, such as being given orders as simply a member of the crew, their careful observance of the world around them lends them a wealth of knowledge, from the classification of whale types to ancient writing about whales. Their expansive knowledge can give them a sense of superiority, albeit often well-deserved.

Compatibility with other personalities

Ishmaels’ open-mindedness allows them to work past any prejudices they may have, and develop a friendship with anyone from any culture, as long as they share a rational, introspective nature. Queequegs, who share Ishmaels’ eye for wonder and agreeable personality, tend to develop friendships – genuine, lasting ones – with Ishmaels.

Strengths

  • Adventurous:
    Ishmaels love the sense of adventure itself and are always ready to take on any situation that comes their way. With a healthy dose of open-mindedness, Ishmaels find delight in the unknown.
  • Imaginitive:
    Life for Ishmael is far more than what meets the eye; Ishmaels see deeper meaning in everything around them. As a result, they are highly creative and a great storyteller.
  • Reflective:
    Constantly reflecting on themselves and those around them, Ishmaels accrue an acute sense of their relationship to the world.

Weaknesses

  • Passive:
    Ishmaels tend to wait for things to come to them; they rarely actively seek out the next step in their life, and scarcely plan ahead to their future aspirations.
  • Egotistical:
    Both observant and reflective, Ishmaels tend to see what others don’t. This can give Ishmaels a feeling of self superiority.
  • Detached:
    Their easy-going nature also means that Ishmaels are rarely invested in the whaling hunt. They’re only along for the ride, and can sometimes even be dismissive or careless about their duties.

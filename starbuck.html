Personalities The Test About

Your personality type is:

Starbuck

Starbucks are reliable, practical mates, who are the first to stand up for what is right.

“I will have no man in my boat who is not afraid of a whale.”

Introduction

The practical, calm, presence of every room, Starbucks are not only reliable friends, but also sensible leaders of their crew. They seek clear, defined roles upon a ship, and thrive in a role that best uses their logical, fact-based thinking. As leaders, Starbucks don’t seek fame nor attention, but rather prefer solid foundations and traditions to base their decisions. They always dedicate themselves to their work and meet their obligations.

Their steadfast ideals of the “proper” way to go about things, however, can sometimes lead to a deadlock with other strong personalities. Starbucks mean what they say and are willing to carry out their beliefs, at any cost necessary – even entertaining the idea of killing a captain he disagrees with. At times like these, Starbucks should attempt to recognize that bluntness may not always be the best way to convince others of their ideas and instead seek to strike a compromise.

Compatibility with other personalities

While Starbucks tend to be highly self-reliant, they can come to develop beneficial relationships with Queequegs, who are equally capable and dedicated to their whaling work. However, Starbucks are often distrustful of others, whether they’re irresponsible like Pip, or irrational like Stubb. They can start or perpetuate conflicts with people whom they disagree with and rarely back down from what they think is right. When confronted with characters like Ahabs, who fundamentally don’t view the world in the logical, factual way that Starbucks do, Starbucks can struggle to resolve conflicts.

Strengths

  • Honest:
    With a strong belief in integrity, Starbucks never hide their intentions or values. They remain calm and clear about what they believe in at all times.
  • Responsible:
    Starbucks are very disciplined, and ensure that they take care of business first. They’d rather lose sleep than fail to carry out their tasks.
  • Practical:
    All of Starbucks’ beliefs are based on observations and fact, and all of Starbucks’ actions are based in logical, moral reasoning. Starbucks are able to see the bigger picture, and strive to make the most rational decisions at all times.

Weaknesses

  • Confrontational:
    When Starbucks recognizes hints of immorality in others, they’re the first ones to point it out; at times, they can be too direct and spark conflict.
  • Judgemental:
    With strong moral opinions, Starbucks are quick to label those with differing opinions as crazy. They can quickly lose respect for those who are willfully ignorant, and such strong judgements can be off-putting.
  • Uncomfortable with change:
    Starbucks love to follow cut-out rules and structured systems; deviations from those can be upsetting to Starbucks. New ideas or differing opinions can leave Starbucks stressed.

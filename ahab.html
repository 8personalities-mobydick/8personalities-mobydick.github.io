Personalities The Test About

Your personality type is:

Ahab

Ahabs are strong-willed, independent captains, who know how to motivate and inspire those around them.

“What I've dared, I've willed; and what I've willed, I'll do!”

Introduction

Ahabs are captains of the ship and would be very unhappy being any other member of the crew. They are highly opinionated, driven, and persistent, and will stop at nothing to catch their “Moby Dick”. While they have some moments of reflection, this doesn’t stop them from acting rashly, often blinded by their ambitions. However, they shouldn’t be mistaken for mindless power-chasers – their ability to find religious and spiritual meaning in something as mundane as fish or see the greater characteristics of humanity in their goals lends them influence over their crew and gives meaning to their goals.

Despite being among a whole crew of capable seamen, Ahabs prefer to take important tasks into their own hands, often watching for whales from the mast themselves or taking their own boat out onto the sea. However, they should be wary about spreading themselves too thin; sometimes, their need for control over every part of their ship can come at the cost of their health, or even their sanity.

Compatibility with other personalities

Ahabs can often successfully forge many lasting, professional relationships, especially with more flexible and easy-going personalities, such as Stubbs and Carpenters. These relationships will be crucial for Ahabs, as their goals are often too ambitious for them to carry out alone. While Ahabs take advantage of many of these relationships in their life to realize their goals, they form true bonds with very few. A Pip – someone equally free-spirited and imaginative, but less ambitious – can often bring out the humanity in Ahabs and help them take a step back from their goals. Close relationships with Pips can help Ahabs open up to other personalities as well, and steer their ambitions towards good.

Strengths

  • Motivated:
    Ahabs’ never-ending ambitions can seem inhuman at times. If directed towards the right cause, Ahabs can change the world – if not, they can throw themselves and everyone else overboard.
  • Strong-willed:
    Self-reliant and confident in their abilities, Ahabs are the unbending forces on every crew. They are willing to put their life on the line for what they believe in.
  • Direct:
    Ahabs are too busy charging towards their goal to be manipulative or secretive. When Ahabs reveal their opinions and goals, you can be sure that they’re telling the truth.

Weaknesses

  • Narrow-minded:
    Ahabs struggle to realize that their ideals aren’t always the right ones, and are quick to feel personally attacked in the face of criticism. While they might see all the signs of impending failure, Ahabs often refuse to give up until it’s too late.
  • Lacking self-awareness:
    With single-minded goals and few genuine connections with others, Ahabs tend to struggle with introspection and recognizing their faults.
  • Difficulty connecting with others:
    Their ambitions lead Ahabs to easily throw away their relationships in favor of greater goals. Ahabs should be careful to not isolate themselves, especially from those who just want the best for them.
